How it works

From tremor sensing to patterned stimulation — in four steps

1) Analyze

The wristband senses tremor patterns using on‑device motion sensors.

2) Align

Electrodes sit over wrist nerves (median & radial) to deliver gentle pulses.

3) Target

Patterns are designed to engage peripheral pathways that relate to tremor circuits (e.g., thalamic VIM‑related pathways).

4) Session

User‑initiated, short sessions with on‑device guidance. Effects and responses may vary.*

*Informational only. No medical claims. Any clinical use depends on local regulations and future evaluations.

Questions

FAQ

Who might consider using the wristband?

People with essential tremor or Parkinson’s tremor who want a non‑invasive, practical option to explore as part of pilot programs.

How is it used?

Users initiate short sessions before activities where steadier hands may help. Session timing and patterns are refined during pilots.

How long do effects last?

Responses vary. Early pilots may observe effects across minutes to hours; systematic study is ongoing.

Is there a warranty?

As a prototype under development, formal warranty terms for research units are shared directly with partners. Commercial terms (if applicable) will be announced later.
